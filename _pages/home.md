### About Our Research
Our research focuses on **computational modeling of human multi-omics** data to uncover the mechanisms driving **cardiovascular and other age-related diseases**, aiming to identify potential therapeutic targets for disease prevention and treatment.

Key areas of interest:

- **Aging – somatic mutation – associated diseases**: We are passionate about understanding the biology connecting aging, somatic mutations (in blood, i.e., clonal hematopoiesis, and in other tissues), and their associated diseases. Beyond mechanistically characterizing these links, we aim to discover interventions capable of breaking or modifying them across the three pillars.

- **Multi-modal medical information & machine learning**: We use deep learning for imaging (human and mouse) and LLMs to process patient information from EHR. For both areas, we further integrate genomics data to discover biological insights and advance our understanding of aging–somatic mutation–associated diseases.

- **Omics methods development**: While we apply multi-omics data to biological questions, we actively tackle fundamental methodological challenges inherent to the data itself (e.g., discordance between proteomics platforms, omics instruments, etc.).

Our research is largely strengthened by the extensive human and murine datasets we have access to, and we are deeply grateful to our collaborators and cohort PIs for their trust.
